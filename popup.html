Help

What is Total Recall?

AI chats forget everything the moment you close the tab. Total Recall saves what you decided to a file on your own computer. Next time you start a new chat, Total Recall pastes your past decisions in so the AI remembers.

You stop having to explain yourself every time.

How does it work?

  1. Pick a folder on your computer. Total Recall creates a file called BRAIN.md inside it. Your decisions live there as plain text — you can read or edit it anytime.
  2. Connect an AI provider (Anthropic, OpenAI, OpenRouter, or Ollama). This is the AI that reads your chats and pulls out the decisions. Settings walks you through getting a key.
  3. After a Claude.ai chat, click the Total Recall icon, then Reconnect, then Extract Now. Three clicks per save. Total Recall reads the chat, picks out the real decisions, and saves them to BRAIN.md.

Why three clicks? Browsers drop the folder-access permission every time the popup closes, so we ask you to grant it back. One click. Not zero, but not painful.

Which AI sites work?

Claude.ai — fully supported. Extract Now is verified against the current page layout. Copy Brain works too.

ChatGPT, Gemini, DeepSeek — experimental in v0.1. Their page layouts change without warning. Extract Now may find nothing or miss messages on these sites. Copy Brain still works because it doesn't read the page — it just wraps your BRAIN.md in a platform-specific intro and copies it to your clipboard.

Reliable extraction on all four is on the v0.2 roadmap.

Why does Total Recall need an AI provider?

Why an AI at all?

Imagine you had a long chat with Claude — 50 messages mixing real decisions, small talk, and what-ifs. If Total Recall just saved the whole chat, BRAIN.md would become huge and messy.

We need something that can read the chat and pull out only the parts that matter — your actual decisions and the reasoning behind them. That is what an AI does well.

The AI we call (named Engram) writes a short summary in a fixed format: what you decided, why, and what you rejected. Just the strategic stuff. No fluff.

Why do I need an API key?

To use an AI, you need an account with one of the AI companies. The API key is your account ID. Without it, the AI company doesn't know who you are.

We don't ship a built-in key because every user would share it, which the AI companies don't allow. Each person needs their own.

The good news: most providers give you free credits when you sign up. And each Extract Now costs less than a cent.

Is there a fully free option?

Yes — pick Ollama as your provider. It runs the AI on your own computer. No account, no key, no cost. One-time setup: install Ollama, then pick it from the provider dropdown.

Do I need a big AI model?

No. The job is small and bounded (read a chat, extract decisions, write a short summary). We use small, fast, cheap models on purpose. A 3 billion-parameter local model on your laptop is plenty.

What does each button do?

Extract Now — Reads the AI chat in the current tab. Saves the decisions to BRAIN.md.

Copy Brain — Copies your decision history with the right wrapper for the AI you're on. Paste it into a fresh chat to pick up where you left off.

Reconnect — Your browser drops the folder link when this popup closes. One click puts it back.

Can I encrypt my API key?

Yes — optional. Open Settings and tick "Encrypt my API key with a passphrase". We wrap your key with AES-GCM using a 256-bit key derived from your passphrase via PBKDF2 (100,000 iterations).

You enter the passphrase once per browser session. The derived key sits in browser memory only — never written to disk — until you close the browser.

What this protects against: someone with read-access to your computer's files (malware, another OS user, a forensic dump) can no longer read your API key without your passphrase.

What it does NOT protect against: an attacker who can run code as you (they could capture the passphrase), or modify the extension itself. Encryption is one layer of defence, not a vault.

If you forget the passphrase, your encrypted key is unrecoverable. Disable encryption (you'd need the passphrase), or clear it in Settings and paste a new key.

Where does my data go?

Your decisions stay on your computer. Total Recall has no server. We never see your chats.

The only outside connection happens when you click Extract Now. The current chat gets sent to the AI provider you chose so they can read it and pull out the decisions. Nothing else leaves your machine.

If you want zero outside connections, pick Ollama as your provider — it runs an AI on your own computer with no cloud at all.

What does it cost?

Cloud providers cost less than a cent per Extract Now. A heavy user might spend 30 cents a month.

Ollama is free after you install it.

When should I use it?

  • After a long planning conversation where you made real decisions
  • When you finish a deep brainstorm or working session
  • Before closing a tab with important context
  • Any time you'd otherwise have to re-explain context in your next chat

Common questions

Why does it say "reconnect needed"?
Your browser drops folder access when the popup closes. Tap Reconnect — one click and you're back.

"No decisions in this chat worth saving" — The chat was just informational, with no real Chose/Why/Rejected decisions. Not a bug.

Why did Extract Now find nothing on ChatGPT/Gemini/DeepSeek?
Those platforms are experimental in v0.1 — see the section above. Try Copy Brain instead, or wait for v0.2.

Can I read BRAIN.md without the extension?
Yes. It's plain markdown. Open it in any text editor or markdown reader.

Can I edit BRAIN.md by hand?
Yes. It's your file. Total Recall will keep appending below your edits.
